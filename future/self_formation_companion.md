# Self-Formation Companion (Future Application Surface)

> **Status: stub — explicit non-deliverable for Stage 1.** This page describes a planned application surface that ships in its own dedicated session. The 15 Stratum meta-skills apply to self-assessment + self-development with explicit Dunning-Kruger guardrails.

---

## What this is

The same Formation framework applied to yourself — assessing your own current capacity across the 15 meta-skills, then developing the gaps.

Self-application is the highest-leverage use of Stratum (you have full information about yourself, you control your own practice contexts, the feedback loops are immediate) and the most error-prone (Dunning-Kruger says self-report is structurally blind to development need; the people who most need to develop a meta-skill are the ones most likely to rate themselves high on it).

So the Self-Formation Companion is built around guardrails that the workplace context handles structurally — third-party verification, behavioral evidence demanded, self-report rejected as primary input.

---

## What this is NOT (yet)

This is not currently shipped content. The Stage 1 audit + Selection + Forging Playbooks operate in workplace context with manager / interviewer / peer as the assessor. The Self-Formation Companion gets its own dedicated session — its own protocol design, its own Dunning-Kruger countermeasures, its own evidence ledger entries.

---

## What the dedicated session would produce

When Self-Formation Companion ships (post-Stage-1), the deliverables would include:

- **Self-assessment protocol with Dunning-Kruger guardrails** — every self-rated score requires (a) at least one specific behavioral example with situation + action + outcome, (b) at least one third-party check (peer, manager, mentor, partner) before the score is recorded, (c) explicit "what would change my score" pre-commitment
- **Adversarial-persona self-tests** — pre-built scenarios that probe specific failure modes in self-assessment (the "I'm decisive" claim with no actual decisions, the "I'm resilient" claim built on endurance not recovery, the "I'm influential" claim about people who already agreed with you)
- **90-day self-formation sprints** per meta-skill — practice contexts in your daily work + life, weekly self-reflection cadence, monthly third-party calibration, quarterly re-assessment
- **Personal evidence ledger** — your own mode classification (trait_claim, partial, demonstrated) per skill, evidence per claim, refinement history
- **Dunning-Kruger countermeasures specific to each meta-skill** — common self-deception patterns per skill and how to surface them
- **Integration with Selection / Forging Playbooks** — how self-assessment connects to (and is checked by) being assessed by others

---

## Why this is deferred to its own session

Self-formation is harder than workplace assessment because the assessor and assessee are the same person. Without explicit guardrails — third-party verification, behavioral evidence requirements, adversarial probes — self-formation collapses into self-flattery dressed as growth.

The Stage 1 audit produces the architecture and the Selection Playbook (assessor → assessee, both clearly separated). The Self-Formation Companion needs its own focused session to design protocols that handle the unique failure mode of self-assessment without losing the framework's core rigor.
