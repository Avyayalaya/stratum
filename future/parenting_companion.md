# Parenting Companion (Future Application Surface)

> **Status: stub — explicit non-deliverable for Stage 1.** This page describes a planned application surface that ships in its own dedicated session. The 15 Stratum meta-skills carry across; the protocols, questions, and dose-response calibration adapt to childhood / adolescent / young-adult contexts.

---

## What this is

The same Formation framework — Selecting (seeing what's truly there) and Forging (growing capacity over time) — applied to raising kids who find their way in the world and thrive.

The 15 meta-skills (First Principles Thinking, Strategic Patience, Resilience, Trust-Building, Cognitive Decoupling, all of them) are human-formation primitives. They apply just as much to a parent reading their kid through their first failure as to a CEO assessing a co-founder. The framework's structural logic — three domains × Person↔Problem / Person↔Adversity / Person↔People interfaces — holds across contexts.

What changes between workplace and home: the questions, the observable behaviors per developmental stage, and the dose-response calibration (childhood → adolescence → young adulthood operates on a years-and-decades cadence, not 90-day sprints).

---

## What this is NOT (yet)

This is not currently shipped content. The framework's architecture audit + workplace-context Selection + Forging Playbooks are Stage 1's deliverables. The Parenting Companion gets its own dedicated session — its own audit, its own playbook structure, its own evidence ledger entries.

---

## What the dedicated session would produce

When Parenting Companion ships (post-Stage-1), the deliverables would include:

- **Age-calibrated question sets** per meta-skill — what does Resilience look like at 7 vs. 14 vs. 21? What's the practice context for Cognitive Decoupling in a teenager? What questions probe Bayesian Updating in an 11-year-old?
- **Observable behaviors per developmental stage** — concrete cues a parent can read in everyday situations (school transitions, friendship ruptures, first failures, first ethical choices)
- **Dose-response on a years-and-decades scale** — how to think about deliberate practice for a meta-skill across childhood; the "90-day sprint" model from the workplace doesn't transfer
- **Parent-self meta-skills** — the parent's own Cognitive Decoupling, Strategic Patience, Confidence Calibration become the substrate; you can't develop in your kid what you can't model
- **Anti-patterns** — what the framework explicitly warns against (over-coaching, performance theater, achievement-as-identity, etc.)
- **Cross-domain transfer notes** — which workplace insights about a meta-skill transfer cleanly to parenting, which need re-derivation, and which fail entirely

---

## Why this is deferred to its own session

The workplace audit (Stage 1) has its own first-principles work to do — the structure, the scoring rubric, the function × level weighting, the evidence ledger. Folding parenting-context content into the workplace audit would either dilute the workplace work or under-build the parenting work.

When Parenting Companion gets its dedicated session, it should be entered with: the audited workplace architecture as a stable foundation, room to interrogate what transfers and what doesn't, and the time to build a parenting-specific evidence ledger that draws on developmental psychology, attachment research, and lived experience.
