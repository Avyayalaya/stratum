# Ledger: Scoring Rubric (1–5)

> **Status: audited (v1.0) — 2026-07-06.** Supporting evidence and counter-evidence triggers populated in the Phase 1.1 architecture audit; every citation DOI-verified via CrossRef. Counter-evidence is not a disclaimer — it operationalizes the framework's stance that literature is a feeder, not a ratifier. The triggers state the conditions under which this decision gets revised.

## Decision

Stratum scores each meta-skill on a 1–5 scale:

- **5 — Demonstrated.** Specific situation, clear actions, measurable outcome. Would mentor others.
- **4 — Partial.** Good evidence but incomplete — missing outcome or context depth.
- **3 — Developing.** Shows awareness and some practice; limited complexity or stakes.
- **2 — Trait Claim.** Claims the skill but provides no behavioral evidence.
- **1 — Insufficient / Avoidance.** Cannot provide an example, deflects, or describes opposite behavior.

The rubric pairs with the Evidence Quality Gate: any score ≥4 must rest on a specific behavioral story (situation + action + outcome). Trait claims fail the gate and score 2 by default, regardless of conviction in delivery.

## Counter-Evidence Triggers

| Trigger | Threshold | Action |
|---------|-----------|--------|
| Prior interview-validity estimates were inflated by overcorrection for range restriction; corrected operational validity is materially lower (Sackett, Zhang, Berry & Lievens 2022, [10.1037/apl0000994](https://doi.org/10.1037/apl0000994)). | If the criterion-related validity of behavioral-evidence scoring is closer to ~.3 than ~.5. | Do not position the 1–5 rubric as near-cognitive-test predictive; frame it as a structured judgment aid, not a validated predictor. |
| Structured behavioral interviews are fakeable — candidates fabricate plausible Situation-Action-Outcome stories, and coaching / social-desirability variance survives added structure (Levashina & Campion 2007, [10.1037/0021-9010.92.6.1638](https://doi.org/10.1037/0021-9010.92.6.1638)). | Any score ≥4 resting on a single unverifiable self-narrated story; systematic inflation for coached or repeat candidates. | Require a verification/probing protocol (contradiction probes, third-party corroboration) before a story earns ≥4; rotate question banks and weight harder-to-rehearse probes. Treat polish as a flag to probe, not a signal to reward. |
