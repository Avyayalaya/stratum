# Stratum Hiring Playbook
## How to Interview for Meta-Skills and Build Elite Teams

> *"Hire for what people do under pressure, not for what they are on paper — because what they do can be developed, and what they are cannot."*

---

## Why This Playbook Exists

Most hiring processes over-index on technical skills and résumé credentials. These are entry bars, not differentiators. The research is clear:

- **Google Project Aristotle** showed team performance is driven by behavioral dynamics — not technical composition.
- **Schmidt & Hunter (1998)** established that structured behavioral interviews are as predictive as cognitive ability tests.
- **Soto et al. (2023)** showed behavioral skills add incremental validity over Big Five personality traits.
- **NASA Astronaut Selection** uses technical ability as a filter, then selects entirely on behavioral meta-skills.
- **Dunning-Kruger** tells us self-report is structurally blind to development need — you must observe behavior, not accept claims.

This playbook gives you the questions, rubrics, and team-composition logic to hire for what actually predicts performance over a 5+ year horizon: **behavioral meta-skills**.

---

## The Three-Domain Framework

Every performance failure that doesn't trace to technical incompetence traces to one of three interfaces:

| Domain | Interface | What It Governs |
|--------|-----------|-----------------|
| **Cognitive Mastery** | Person ↔ Problem | How they think under complexity and uncertainty |
| **Character Core** | Person ↔ Adversity | How they act under pressure, failure, and ethical ambiguity |
| **Trust Dynamics** | Person ↔ People | How they build influence, alignment, and credibility across teams |

The ordering is causal: cognition provides the reasoning substrate; character allows it to operate under real pressure; trust dynamics multiplies individual capability across team systems.

## Interview Questions by Meta-Skill

For each skill, ask **one primary question** and use the **probing follow-ups** when the answer is terse or lacks specifics. You're looking for **behavioral evidence** — concrete situations, actions taken, and outcomes — not trait claims ("I'm a good decision-maker").

### 1. First Principles Thinking
**Primary:** "Tell me about a time you had to solve a problem no one had solved before. How did you approach it?"
- *Probe:* "What assumptions did you question?"
- *Probe:* "How did you decide what was fundamental vs. inherited convention?"
- *Red flag:* They describe following an existing playbook without questioning it.

### 2. Decision-Making Under Uncertainty
**Primary:** "Describe a high-stakes decision you made with incomplete information. Walk me through it."
- *Probe:* "What did you know, and what were you guessing?"
- *Probe:* "How did you know when you had enough to decide?"
- *Red flag:* They waited for perfect information or deferred the decision entirely.

### 3. Scenario Thinking
**Primary:** "When planning something complex, how do you prepare for things going wrong?"
- *Probe:* "Can you give me a specific example where you mapped out multiple possible outcomes?"
- *Probe:* "What early signals were you watching for?"
- *Red flag:* They planned for only the happy path.

### 4. Feedback Calibration
**Primary:** "Tell me about a time you received feedback that changed how you approached something."
- *Probe:* "How quickly did you act on it?"
- *Probe:* "Was there feedback you initially disagreed with but later accepted?"
- *Red flag:* Defensiveness, or no concrete example of iteration.

### 5. Bayesian Updating
**Primary:** "Describe a situation where your initial belief or plan turned out to be wrong. What did you do?"
- *Probe:* "How did you decide how much to adjust?"
- *Probe:* "Did you over-correct or under-correct? How do you know?"
- *Red flag:* They either anchored rigidly or swung 180° without reasoning.

### 6. Strategic Patience
**Primary:** "Tell me about a time when waiting — not acting — led to a better outcome."
- *Probe:* "How did you manage pressure from others to move faster?"
- *Probe:* "How did you distinguish patience from avoidance?"
- *Red flag:* They can't separate inaction from intentional delay.

### 7. Resilience
**Primary:** "Tell me about a significant professional setback. What did you do in the first 72 hours?"
- *Probe:* "How did you restore your energy and clarity?"
- *Probe:* "What did you learn that you still apply today?"
- *Red flag:* The story is about enduring, not about recovering and growing.

### 8. Strength of Character
**Primary:** "When have you made a decision that you knew would be unpopular but believed was right?"
- *Probe:* "What did it cost you?"
- *Probe:* "Would you do it again?"
- *Red flag:* They describe compliance with policy, not independent moral judgment.

### 9. Cognitive Decoupling
**Primary:** "Describe a moment when you felt triggered or emotionally charged at work. How did you handle it?"
- *Probe:* "What was the gap between your first impulse and your actual response?"
- *Probe:* "How do others experience you in tense moments?"
- *Red flag:* They claim they "never get emotional" (self-awareness gap).

### 10. Execution with Reversibility
**Primary:** "Tell me about a bold move you made that had a built-in exit or rollback plan."
- *Probe:* "How did you design the reversibility?"
- *Probe:* "Did you end up needing it?"
- *Red flag:* They shipped something irreversible without considering failure paths.

### 11. Trust-Building
**Primary:** "How do you build trust with someone who doesn't know you yet — especially in a high-stakes context?"
- *Probe:* "What's the difference between being liked and being trusted?"
- *Probe:* "Tell me about a time trust broke. What did you do?"
- *Red flag:* They describe trust as a personality trait, not a set of behaviors.

### 12. Ability to Influence
**Primary:** "Describe a time you shifted a decision or direction without having formal authority."
- *Probe:* "How did you sequence the buy-in?"
- *Probe:* "What resistance did you face, and how did you navigate it?"
- *Red flag:* They escalated instead of influencing, or they claim it "just happened."

### 13. Narrative Framing
**Primary:** "Tell me about a time you had to reframe a negative outcome into something productive."
- *Probe:* "What story did you tell — and to whom?"
- *Probe:* "How did the framing change the team's energy or direction?"
- *Red flag:* They describe spin rather than genuine sense-making.

### 14. Tribal Intelligence
**Primary:** "Describe entering a politically sensitive environment. What did you observe first, and how did you navigate it?"
- *Probe:* "How did you figure out who held real influence?"
- *Probe:* "How did you adapt your approach to different groups?"
- *Red flag:* They treated every stakeholder identically, missing group dynamics entirely.

### 15. Confidence Calibration
**Primary:** "When have you said 'I don't know' in a high-stakes meeting — and how was it received?"
- *Probe:* "How do you signal confidence without overstating certainty?"
- *Probe:* "How do you make it easy for others to calibrate your input?"
- *Red flag:* They perform certainty on everything, or they hide behind false humility.

## Scoring Rubric

Rate each skill 1–5 based on the quality of **behavioral evidence** in their response:

| Score | Label | What You Heard |
|-------|-------|---------------|
| 5 | **Demonstrated** | Specific situation, clear actions, measurable outcome. Would mentor others. |
| 4 | **Partial** | Good evidence but incomplete — missing outcome or context depth. |
| 3 | **Developing** | Shows awareness and some practice, but limited complexity or stakes. |
| 2 | **Trait Claim** | Claims the skill ("I'm decisive") but provides no behavioral evidence. |
| 1 | **Insufficient / Avoidance** | Can't provide an example, deflects, or describes the opposite behavior. |

### Evidence Quality Gate
- **Accept:** "Last quarter, when our pilot data came back negative, I revised our roadmap assumptions within 48 hours and presented three options to leadership."
- **Reject:** "I'm very adaptable and always update my thinking." (This is a trait claim, not evidence.)

## Team Composition Logic

After scoring individuals, use these principles to compose or diagnose teams:

### 1. Coverage Check
Every team needs at least one person scoring ≥4 in each domain. A team of brilliant thinkers (high Cognitive Mastery) who can't build trust will fail on execution.

### 2. Blind Spot Detection
If **no one** on the team scores above 3 in a specific skill, that's a structural blind spot. Common patterns:
- All high Cognitive Mastery, low Trust Dynamics → smart people who can't collaborate
- All high Character Core, low Cognitive Mastery → principled people who make poor decisions
- All high Trust Dynamics, low Character Core → likeable people who fold under pressure

### 3. Conflict Risk Pairs
Watch for combinations that create friction:
- Low Trust-Building + Low Cognitive Decoupling → interpersonal explosions under stress
- Low Confidence Calibration + High Influence → persuasive but unreliable signal
- Low First Principles + High Narrative Framing → compelling stories built on weak logic

### 4. Complementary Strengths
Pair people deliberately:
- High First Principles + High Narrative Framing → one sees truth, the other communicates it
- High Resilience + High Strategic Patience → one recovers, the other holds timing
- High Tribal Intelligence + High Influence → one maps the terrain, the other moves through it

### 5. Hiring Recommendation
When your team has a blind spot, screen your next hire explicitly for that gap. Use the behavioral questions above — don't add a new personality test. **One framework, two use cases: development and hiring.**

## Role-Weighted Skill Prioritization

Not all 15 skills matter equally for every role and level. A gap in First Principles is disqualifying for a senior engineer but marginal for a sales leader. A gap in Tribal Intelligence barely registers for an IC but will end a VP's career.

### How It Works

**Function** (what you do) determines WHICH skills matter most. **Level** (scope of impact) determines HOW MUCH they matter. Combined, they give you the hiring bar and development priorities calibrated for the actual job.

### Weight Scale
| Weight | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| **3.0 — Defining** | Separates extraordinary from average. A gap here is disqualifying. |
| **2.0 — Critical** | Expected at high proficiency. A gap causes visible failure. |
| **1.5 — Important** | Contributes meaningfully. A gap limits the person's ceiling. |
| **1.0 — Baseline** | Minimum competency. Not a differentiator. |

### Level Transitions — What Changes at Each Step

| Level Shift | What Changes |
|-------------|-------------|
| **IC → Senior IC** | Autonomy rises. First Principles and Decision-Making increase. |
| **Senior IC → Manager** | **Biggest shift.** You now operate through others. Trust-Building and Cognitive Decoupling jump sharply. |
| **Manager → Director** | Portfolio view. Scenario Thinking, Strategic Patience, and Narrative Framing rise. |
| **Director → VP** | Organizational shaping. Influence, Tribal Intelligence, and Confidence Calibration become defining. |
| **VP → C-Suite** | Institutional identity. Strength of Character and all Trust Dynamics skills are defining. |

### Function Profiles (Defining + Critical Skills)

| Function | Defining (3.0) | Critical (2.0) |
|----------|----------------|----------------|
| **Product Management** | Decision Under Uncertainty, Influence | Scenario Thinking, Narrative Framing, Bayesian Updating |
| **Software Engineering** | First Principles, Feedback Calibration | Execution w/ Reversibility, Decision Under Uncertainty |
| **Design** | Feedback Calibration, Narrative Framing | First Principles, Influence |
| **Data Science / ML** | First Principles, Bayesian Updating | Confidence Calibration, Feedback Calibration |
| **Sales / BD** | Influence, Trust-Building, Resilience | Tribal Intelligence, Confidence Calibration, Strategic Patience |
| **Marketing** | Narrative Framing, Bayesian Updating | Influence, Scenario Thinking |
| **Operations** | Execution w/ Reversibility, Decision Under Uncertainty | Scenario Thinking, First Principles, Cognitive Decoupling |
| **People / HR** | Trust-Building, Strength of Character, Cognitive Decoupling | Influence, Tribal Intelligence, Narrative Framing |
| **Finance / Strategy** | Decision Under Uncertainty, Bayesian Updating | Scenario Thinking, Strength of Character, Confidence Calibration |
| **General Management** | Influence, Narrative Framing, Trust-Building | Cognitive Decoupling, Decision Under Uncertainty, Tribal Intelligence |
| **Founder** | Decision Under Uncertainty, Resilience, Influence | First Principles, Bayesian Updating, Execution w/ Reversibility, Narrative Framing |

### Minimum Viable Profile — Hire/No-Hire Gates

A score ≤ 2 in any gate skill is a **hard no** — no other strength compensates.

| Function | IC Gate Skills | Manager Gate Skills (adds to IC) | Director+ Gate Skills (adds to Manager) |
|----------|---------------|----------------------------------|----------------------------------------|
| **Product** | Decision Under Uncertainty, First Principles | + Influence, Narrative Framing | + Scenario Thinking, Tribal Intelligence |
| **Engineering** | First Principles, Feedback Calibration | + Trust-Building, Cognitive Decoupling | + Decision Under Uncertainty, Confidence Calibration |
| **Design** | Feedback Calibration, First Principles | + Influence, Narrative Framing | + Resilience, Trust-Building |
| **Data/ML** | First Principles, Bayesian Updating | + Confidence Calibration, Narrative Framing | + Decision Under Uncertainty, Influence |
| **Sales** | Resilience, Trust-Building | + Influence, Tribal Intelligence | + Strategic Patience, Narrative Framing |
| **Marketing** | Narrative Framing, Feedback Calibration | + Bayesian Updating, Influence | + Scenario Thinking, Decision Under Uncertainty |
| **Operations** | Execution w/ Reversibility, Decision Under Uncertainty | + Scenario Thinking, Cognitive Decoupling | + Trust-Building, Strategic Patience |
| **People/HR** | Trust-Building, Cognitive Decoupling | + Strength of Character, Influence | + Tribal Intelligence, Narrative Framing |
| **Finance** | Decision Under Uncertainty, Bayesian Updating | + Strength of Character, Scenario Thinking | + Confidence Calibration, Narrative Framing |
| **Gen. Management** | Influence, Trust-Building | + Cognitive Decoupling, Narrative Framing | + Tribal Intelligence, Strategic Patience |

**Universal gate (all functions):** A Manager-level candidate scoring ≤ 2 on Trust-Building is a hard no regardless of function. You cannot manage without trust.

### Gap Calculation

For each skill:
1. **Function weight** = the tier from the function profile (Defining = 3.0, Critical = 2.0, Important = 1.5, other = 1.0)
2. **Level modifier** = how much this skill matters at this level (see level transition logic — Trust Dynamics skills multiply steeply from IC to VP+)
3. **Gap score** = (5 − candidate score) × function weight × level modifier
4. Sort gaps descending. **Top 3 = the 90-day development sprint.** Any gate skill ≤ 2 = hard no.

## How to Run a Full Assessment

### For Hiring (Interview)
1. Identify the candidate's **function** and **level**
2. Check the Minimum Viable Profile gates — these are the non-negotiable skills to assess
3. Add the defining and critical skills from the function profile (typically 5–8 total)
4. Ask the primary question for each; probe for specifics — you want stories, not claims
5. Score using the rubric immediately after the conversation
6. Check gates first: any gate skill ≤ 2 = hard no
7. Calculate gap scores for remaining skills; compare across candidates on the same weighted basis

### For Team Development (Coaching)
1. Assess all 15 skills per team member (self + peer + manager)
2. Generate a team heatmap — who covers what
3. Identify blind spots and conflict risk pairs
4. Build 90-day development sprints targeting top 2–3 gaps per person
5. Re-assess quarterly to track growth

### For Team Formation (Project Staffing)
1. Define the challenge type: high-ambiguity? high-conflict? high-speed?
2. Map the critical meta-skills for that challenge
3. Staff for coverage, not uniformity
4. Brief the team on their complementary strengths

## What Makes This Different

1. **Behavioral evidence, not self-report.** The questions are designed to elicit what people *did*, not what they *claim to be*. The scoring rubric rejects trait claims structurally.

2. **Mode classification tells you WHY someone scored low.** A score of 2 with "trait claim" means they talk the talk. A score of 2 with "avoidance" means they're choosing comfort. A score of 2 with "insufficient" means they might be great but can't articulate it. Different diagnosis → different intervention.

3. **Team-level intelligence.** Individual scores are inputs. The real output is: where is this team blind, where will it break, and what should you hire for next?

4. **One framework for hiring AND development.** Use the same questions and rubrics whether you're interviewing a candidate or coaching a direct report.

5. **Research-backed, not invented.** Every design choice traces to published evidence on team performance, behavioral assessment validity, and self-report limitations.
